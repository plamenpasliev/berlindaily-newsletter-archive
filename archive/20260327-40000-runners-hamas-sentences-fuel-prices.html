🏃‍♂️ 40,000 Runners Hit Berlin This Sunday

The 45th Berlin Half Marathon returns this Sunday, bringing significant traffic disruptions to the city center. More than 40,000 participants from over 130 nations will navigate the 21.097-kilometer course. The main event starts near the Brandenburg Gate, while Saturday features a new 1.6-kilometer Garmin Berlin Mile race. (Tagesspiegel)

The main race begins Sunday at 9:15 a.m. with inline skaters, followed by wheelchair athletes and the massive running field. German record holder Amanal Petros, who set his 59:31 personal best in Berlin last year, leads the men's elite contenders. In the women's race, Ethiopian runner Likina Amebaw enters as the top favorite with a personal best of 1:04:44. (rbb24)

Preparations will shut down the Straße des 17. Juni and surrounding Tiergarten streets starting Thursday morning. The entire route will be blocked to motorized traffic by 7:45 a.m. on Sunday, remaining closed until late afternoon. The Traffic Information Centre advises drivers to bypass the inner city via the A100 or Tiergarten Tunnel and recommends using the U-Bahn and S-Bahn. (berlin.de)

⚖️ Berlin Court Sentences Four Hamas Operatives

A Berlin court sentenced four men to prison terms ranging from four-and-a-half to six years for stockpiling weapons across Europe on behalf of Hamas. The defendants were convicted of membership in a foreign terrorist organization after acting as operatives for the Qassam Brigades. Judges confirmed the men managed underground firearms caches in Poland, Bulgaria, and Denmark to prepare for future attacks. (DW.com)

Authorities arrested the men in December 2023 after receiving intelligence regarding a conspiratorial operation in Germany. Prosecutors identified the Israeli embassy in Berlin and the United States Ramstein airbase as potential targets. The main defendant admitted visiting a Bulgarian arms cache but unsuccessfully argued the trip was solely for private weapons dealing. (Spiegel)

The landmark ruling formally affirms Hamas's classification as a terrorist organization within a German state security trial. European intelligence agencies have escalated operations to disrupt militant financing and logistics since the arrests. Authorities recently executed additional cross-border detentions, signaling a sustained regional campaign to dismantle extremist support networks operating outside the Middle East. (The National)

⛽ Fuel Price Hikes Limited to Once a Day

German lawmakers passed legislation restricting petrol stations to a single daily price increase, permitted only at midday, to combat fuel costs driven by the Iran war. Price reductions remain allowed at any time. Violators face fines of up to €100,000 under the rules, which were modeled on the Austrian system and backed by Chancellor Friedrich Merz’s coalition. (Yahoo)

The policy responds to extreme price volatility that complicates consumer choices. The Federal Cartel Office reports that stations average 20 price changes daily, peaking at 50, which undermines price comparison apps. Meanwhile, independent stations argue the true burden stems from high state taxes and carbon levies rather than market manipulation. (DIE ZEIT)

Officials admit the daily cap may not offer enough financial relief, prompting the coalition to consider stronger market interventions. A government task force is now weighing a temporary energy tax reduction and a windfall tax on energy companies. Economy Minister Katherina Reiche also voiced support for increasing the commuter allowance. (Global Banking & Finance Review®)
